# Explainable-corrosion-segmentation-framework
 Reliable corrosion monitoring is essential for maintaining structural safety and serviceability of steel structures. However, most existing vision-based corrosion monitoring models designed for bridges and other exterior steel structures often exhibit limited performance in indoor industrial environments due to adverse illumination, uneven corrosion distribution, and cluttered backgrounds. In addition, the explainability of segmentation-based corrosion assessment remains insufficiently investigated. To address these limitations, this study develops an explainable corrosion segmentation framework for high-resolution images of steel components in steel-structured industrial buildings. A dataset containing 195 high-resolution images was established to support corrosion inspection in this challenging indoor scenario. Within the proposed framework, a high-resolution corrosion segmentation pipeline, including an improved semantic segmentation model, was developed to preserve fine-grained corrosion information and enhance pixel-level recognition accuracy. To improve the interpretability of model predictions, a Kernel SHAP-based explanation module was further constructed to quantify the contributions of different image regions to the predicted corrosion response within user-defined regions of interest. Experimental results show that the segmentation model improves the F1 score from 0.62 to 0.71 compared with the baseline while maintaining balance between precision and recall. The proposed framework provides an accurate and interpretable solution for corrosion inspection of steel components in industrial buildings and shows potential for condition assessment and maintenance decision-making in building engineering practice.
